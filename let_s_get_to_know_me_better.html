How and why did I become a UX Designer?

After having spent a couple of years developing SAP Fiori applications, I decided to bridge the gap between a designer and a developer by moving on to a UX Designer's role.

As a developer, I always craved more conversation and the chance to empathise with the users. Given my interest and my capability to also develop Fiori applications I figured that I had a rare combination that could work well for both the users and the development teams (and myself ;)).

There was another huge driving force that led me to where I am now though, and that's the art of storytelling. I've always loved framing elaborate stories for even the smallest of incidents. That innate need to maintain detail and care for every small turn is what I believe makes me a better designer, and a human too maybe?

So I moved on. I'm better off talking to humans than computers!

Why am I best suited for this job?

I have been a part of multiple co-development projects in the past. My niche is my ability to truly focus on the aspects of psychology and physiology. Essentially the "Why's", the "How's" and the "Where's". Though I don't possess a degree in it, my capability to empathise combined with my ability to research, adapt and implement makes me equally good, if not better.

I’ve been working with cross-functional teams for almost a decade now and that's given me the ability to adapt my communication style whenever and wherever needed. Every team has its way of communicating and functioning irrespective of however defined the ways of a boss/company are (let's face it, that's the untold truth), knowing that, learning that and then becoming a part of that makes things just a smidge easier in my opinion.

I've worked and managed development teams directly (with a splash of functional and testing folks, here and there) and that's made me better at understanding everybody's side of things. It has been proven multiple times in the past that it makes me a better designer.

I’m a very vocal and extroverted guy by nature and thrive on effective communication. I believe that conversations can do wonders and solve any conflict or give rise to solutions in even the toughest of problem spaces. This inherent trait has not only defined my success as a leader but has also proven instrumental in my managerial roles. I'd like to think my jovial and easy-to-approach nature helps too. So, want me to lead a team? I've got a proven past that I can.
